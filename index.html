Built by Someone Who Uses It Daily

This isn't a concept.
It's my actual life.

I built Django AI for myself in March 2026. Not as a product — as a tool I needed. I wanted an AI that knew my schedule, read my email, tracked my recovery scores, and lived in iMessage so I'd actually use it.

12 days later, it had 30+ tools. Calendar management, email triage, Whoop health data, a personal knowledge vault, weather that knows when I'm traveling, and morning briefings triggered by my sleep data — not an alarm.

The whole stack runs on a Mac Mini under my desk for $28/month. No subscriptions to cancel. No cloud provider to trust. No app to open. Just iMessage.

Then a friend texted — unprompted — asking if I could build him one. That's when I realized this wasn't just a project. It was a product.

"An ecosystem that compounds beats a chatbot that forgets."

Every conversation your AI has, every piece of data it pulls, every note it saves — it all goes into the same knowledge base. The system on day 30 is categorically different from the system on day 1, not because the model improved, but because the context did.
