Security and privacy

Your data never leaves
your network

We're asking you to trust us with your calendar, email, health data, and contacts. Here's exactly how the system protects it — and the one tradeoff we want you to know about.

"If we can't explain exactly where your data goes, we shouldn't be handling it."

Your hardware, your home

The Mac Mini sits in your house. Calendar, email, contacts, vault, health data — it all lives on that machine. There is no cloud database. No one else has a copy.

Tailscale private network

Your Mac Mini is only accessible through Tailscale, a private mesh VPN. No ports are open to the internet. No one can reach your server without being on your network. Public scanners see nothing.

AI processing via Anthropic API

Conversations are processed by Claude (Anthropic). Anthropic does not train on API data and does not store conversations beyond a 30-day safety window. Your messages are not used to improve their models.

Encrypted sync

Your knowledge vault syncs between devices via Obsidian Sync, which is end-to-end encrypted. Not even Obsidian's team can read your notes. Health data stays local — Whoop credentials are stored on the Mac Mini, never in the cloud.

Transparency note

iMessages between you and the bot route through SendBlue, a third-party API that bridges iMessage. This means message content passes through their servers. SendBlue does not store message content after delivery, but we want you to know about this dependency. We're actively evaluating alternatives to remove this intermediary.
